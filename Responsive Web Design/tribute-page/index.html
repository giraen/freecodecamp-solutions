A Short Timeline of Sir Isaac Newton's Life

  • 1642:
    Isaac Newton was born in Woolsthorpe-by-Colsterworth, Lincolnshire, England.
  • 1661:
    Newton began studying at Trinity College, Cambridge.
  • 1665:
    During the Great Plague, Newton returned to Woolsthorpe and began developing his ideas on calculus, optics, and gravity.
  • 1667:
    He developed a series of mathematical papers called "Method of Fluxions," laying the groundwork for calculus.
  • 1687:
    Newton published his most famous work, "Philosophi√¶ Naturalis Principia Mathematica" (Mathematical Principles of Natural Philosophy), which described his laws of motion and universal gravitation.
  • 1696:
    Newton was appointed Warden of the Royal Mint, a position he held for the rest of his life.
  • 1703:
    He became the President of the Royal Society.
  • 1727:
    Isaac Newton passed away in Kensington, London, England, at the age of 84.
